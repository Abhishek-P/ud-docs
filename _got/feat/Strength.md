Adjectives and participles have so-called strong (indefinite) and weak (definite) inflection.

Note that in other languages, the same phenomenon is annotated using other language-specific
features: [Variant](cs-feat/Variant) (`Variant=Short` is the strong, indefinite form), or
even [Definite]()ness.

### <a name="Strong">`Strong`</a>: strong, indefinite form

#### Examples

* 𐌼𐌰𐌽𐌰𐌲𐌰𐌹 _<b>managai</b>_ “many” (strong masculine plural nominative)

### <a name="Weak">`Weak`</a>: weak, definite form

#### Examples

* 𐌼𐌰𐌽𐌰𐌲𐌰𐌽𐍃 _<b>managans</b>_ “many” (weak masculine plural nominative)
