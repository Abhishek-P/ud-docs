Mood is a feature that expresses modality and subclassifies finite
verb forms.

### <a name="Imp">`Imp`</a>: imperative

The speaker uses imperative to order or ask the addressee to do the action of the verb.

#### Examples
- _bak ʔabk -i -n / w= hi_ "Take the lamb like that!"

### <a name="Opt">`Opt`</a>: optative

Expresses exclamations for a wish like "May you have a long life!" or "If only I were rich!"
In Beja the negative optative is compulsory in dependent clauses.

#### Examples
- _harʔiːsiːsi <b>bi=</b> t- jʔ <b>-a</b> =heːb =aj_ "It must not come to me from behind!"

### <a name="Pot">`Pot`</a>: Potential

#### Examples
- _hinin rhisat =oːk <b>ʔeːnaj</b>_ "We are going to show you!"
