In Classical Armenian, NumType as a lexical feature, which is tagged on [numerals](xcl-pos/NUM), [adjectives](xcl-pos/ADJ) and [adverbs](xcl-pos/ADV). Classical Armenian has a decimal numeral system. Complex numerals are built by means of derivation, compounding, and coordination.

### <a name="Card">`Card`</a>: cardinal numeral

Cardinal numerals modify nominals, with which they partially agreement in number and case. 

#### Examples

* մի/_mi_ “one”, երկու/_erkow_ “two”, երեք/_erekʻ_ “three”

### <a name="Dist">`Dist`</a>: distributive numeral

Used to express that the same quantity is distributed to each member in a set of targets.

#### Examples

* առաքեաց զնոսա <b>երկուս երկուս</b> առաջի իւր / _aṙakʻeacʻ znosa <b>erkows erkows</b> aṙaǰi iwr_ “sent them two and two before his face” (Lk.10:1).

### <a name="Mult">`Mult`</a>: multiplicative numeral

Expresses an entity or quality which occurs a number of times.

#### Examples

* մինչչեւ հաւու խաւսեալ իցէ. <b>երիցս</b> ուրասցիս զիս / _minčʻčʻew hawow xawseal icʻē: <b>ericʻs</b> owrascʻis zis_ “before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice” (Mt.26:34).

### <a name="Ord">`Ord`</a>: ordinal number

Ordinal numerals are classified as [adjectives](xcl-pos/ADJ). Except for առաջին/_aṙaǰin_ “first”, they are derived from cardinals with the suffix -րորդ/_-rord_ and -երորդ/_-erord_.

#### Examples

* առաջին/_aṙaǰin_ “first”, երկրորդ/_erkrord_ “second”, հինգերորդ/_hingerord_ “fifth”

### <a name="Sets">`Sets`</a>: collective numeral

Expresses a number of entities represented as a whole.

#### Examples

կոչեցեալ առ ինքն <b>զերկոտասանեսին</b> աշակերտսն իւր / _kočʻecʻeal aṙ inkʻn <b>zerkotasanesin</b> ašakertsn iwr_ “he had called unto him all of his twelve disciples” (Mt.10:1).
