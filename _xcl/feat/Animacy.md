In Classical Armenian, animacy as a lexical feature is tagged on some [pronouns](xcl-pos/PRON) and pronominal adjectives ([determiners] (xcl-pos/DET)). It has two values, animate vs. inanimate. Unlike <a href="https://universaldependencies.org/hy/feat/Animacy.html">Modern Eastern Armenian</a>, where animacy (`Hum` vs. `Nhum`) controls the case of the object (`Dat` vs. `Acc`, respectively), the opposed values of animacy are only expressed in pronouns and determiners as such in Classical Armenian. Animacy is not tagged on nominals which do not have dedicated morphological forms to express animate vs. inanimate participants.  

### <a name="Anim">`Anim`</a>: animate

Pronouns and pronominal adjectives referring to animate participants.

#### Examples

* ով/_ov_ “who”
* ոք/_okʻ_ “anyone”, “any”
* ոմն/_omn_ “someone”, “certain”

### <a name="Inan">`Inan`</a>: inanimate

Pronouns and pronominal adjectives referring to inanimate participants.

#### Examples

* զինչ/_zinčʻ_ “what”, “which”
* ինչ/_inčʻ_ “something”, “any”
* իմն/_imn_ “certain”
