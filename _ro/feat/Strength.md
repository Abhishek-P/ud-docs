Personal [pronouns](u-pos/PRON) in Romanian have long, stressed forms, and short, clitic forms. The long
forms are also called strong and the clitics are called weak.

Note that in Slavic languages, the same phenomenon is annotated using another language-specific
feature, [Variant](cs-feat/Variant) (`Variant=Short` denotes the weak, clitic form).

### <a name="Strong">`Strong`</a>: strong, stressed, independent form of personal pronoun

#### Examples

* _<b>mine</b>_ “me”, _<b>tine</b>_ “you”, _<b>el, lui</b>_ “him”, _<b>ea</b>_ “her”, _<b>noi</b>_ “us”, _<b>voi</b>_ “you”, _<b>ei, ele, lor</b>_ “them”, _<b>sine</b>_ “oneself”

### <a name="Weak">`Weak`</a>: weak, unstressed, clitic form of personal pronoun

#### Examples

* _<b>mă</b>_ “me”, _<b>te</b>_ “you”, _<b>-l</b>_ “him”, _<b>o</b>_ “her”, _<b>ne</b>_ “us”, _<b>vă</b>_ “you”, _<b>-i, le</b>_ “them”, _<b>să, s-</b>_ “oneself”
