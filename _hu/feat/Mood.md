We apply the features Ind, Cnd and Imp in Hungarian.

### <a name="Ind">`Ind`</a>: indicative

The indicative can be considered the default mood. A verb in
indicative merely states that something happens, has happened or will
happen, without adding any attitude of the speaker.

#### Examples

* [hu] _Az egyetemen <b>tanulsz</b>._ “<b>You study</b> at the university.”

### <a name="Imp">`Imp`</a>: imperative

#### Examples

* [hu] _<b>Tanulj</b> az egyetemen!_ “<b>Study</b> at the university!”

### <a name="Cnd">`Cnd`</a>: conditional

#### Examples

* [hu] _Ha okos lennék, az egyetemen <b>tanulnék</b>._ “If I were smart I <b>would study</b> at the university.”

### <a name="Pot">`Pot`</a>: potential

The action of the verb is possible but not certain.
This mood corresponds to the modal verbs _can, might, be able to_.
In Hungarian, it can be also combined with imperative and conditional,
leading to multivalues `Cnd,Pot` and `Imp,Pot`.

#### Examples

* [hu] _<b>Tanulhatok</b> az egyetemen._ “I <b>can study</b> at the university.”
* [hu] _<b>Tanulhatnék</b> az egyetemen._ “I <b>could study</b> at the university.” `Mood=Cnd,Pot`
* [hu] _hogy az egyetemen <b>tanulhasson</b>_ “so that <b>you can study</b> at the university” `Mood=Imp,Pot`
