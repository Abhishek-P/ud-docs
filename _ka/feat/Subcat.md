The `Subcat` feature applies to [VERB](_ka/pos/VERB). Georgian verb can be intransitive, indirect transitive and transitive.

### <a name="Intr">`Intr`</a>: intransitive verb

A verb that does not take arguments other than the subject.

#### Examples

* _აფართხალდა_ 'he/she/it trembled', _აფართხალდნენ_ 'they trembled' etc.

### <a name="Indir">`Indir`</a>: indirect transitive verb

A verb that does not require a direct object but it requires an oblique argument.

#### Examples

* _ხვდება_ 'he/she/it meets him/her/it/them', _ხვდებიან_ 'they meet him/her/it/them' etc.

### <a name="Tran">`Tran`</a>: transitive verb

A verb that takes a direct object as argument (in addition to the subject). 

#### Examples

* _აშენებს_ 'he/she/it builds smth.', _აშენებენ_ 'they build smth.' etc.
