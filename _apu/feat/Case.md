Case is an inflectional feature for [nouns](u-pos/NOUN), [pronouns](u-pos/PRON)and [adjectives](u-pos/ADJ)
 in Apurinã.<br/>
Apurinã has 5 inflectional cases.


### <a name="Com">`Com`</a>: comitative / associative

The comitative (also called associative) case corresponds to English
"together with &hellip;" `-kata`.

#### Examples

* [apu] _<b>ytãnurukata</b>_ "with his wife"

### <a name="Dat">`Dat`</a>: dative

The dative case expresses transfer to someone.
`-munhi`

#### Examples

* [apu] _<b>ywamunhi</b>_ "to him"

### <a name="Loc">`Loc`</a>: locative

The locative case expesses both spatial stationary and goal with `-ã`.
Additionally it can be used to express instrument.

#### Examples

* [apu] _<b>aapukutxiã</b>_ "in his house"


### <a name="Nom">`Nom`</a>: nominative

The base form of the noun, this is the form with no case formative.
It is used for both subject and object marking.

#### Examples

* [apu] _<b>Ywa</b> matxaka kaiãapuku <b>maky</b>._ "He took a lot of nuts."

### <a name="Tem">`Tem`</a>: temporal

The temporal case is used to indicate duration or point in time `-saaky`

#### Examples

* [apu] _<b>Ĩkanapyryãkasaaky</b>_ "when he returned"
