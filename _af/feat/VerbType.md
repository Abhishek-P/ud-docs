The `VerbType` feature is used in Afrikaans to further distinguish subtypes of auxiliary verbs.

### <a name="Cop">`Cop`</a>: copula verb

Verb used to make nominal predicates from adjectives, nouns or participles.

#### Examples

* _Ons demokrasie <b>is</b> gesond._ “Our democracy is healthy.”

### <a name="Aux">`Aux`</a>: temporal auxiliary verb

Verb used to create periphrastic perfect tenses.

#### Examples

* _Dit <b>het</b> tot 'n verenigde staat gelei._ “This has led to a united state.”

### <a name="Pas">`Pas`</a>: passive auxiliary verb

Verb used to create periphrastic passive voice: _word_ (present) and _wees_ (past).

#### Examples

* _Welvaart <b>word</b> geskep om mense se lewenskwaliteit te verbeter._ “Wealth is created to improve people's quality of life.”

### <a name="Mod">`Mod`</a>: modal verb

A group of auxiliary verbs that alter the mood of the clause (necessity, potentiality etc.)
The future tense auxiliary is also considered modal.

#### Examples

* _Deur saam te werk, <b>kan</b> ons meer bereik._ “By working together, we can achieve more.”
