As there are different types of particles ([PART]()) in Afrikaans, we indicate which type they are.



### <a name="Gen">`Gen`</a>: genitival _se_

The particle _se_ introducing an genitive. Actually the possessor is following this particle, while the possessed item is preceding it.

#### Examples

* _Hierdie en ander programme, insluitend die Apex-Prioriteite wat in verlede Februarie <b>se</b> Staatsrede geïdentifiseer is, vorm die basis van ons pogings om aan ons mandaat te voldoen en die grondwerk vir die toekoms te doen._ “These and other programmes, including the Apex Priorities which have been identified in the State of the Nation of last February, are the base of our attemps to fulfil our mandate and do the groundwork for the future.”


### <a name="Inf">`Inf`</a>: infinitival _te_

The particle _te_ introducing an infinitive.

#### Examples

* _En ons hoop hierdie keer dat hernude pogings deur die internasionale gemeenskap om blywende oplossings vir hierdie konflik <b>te</b> vind, vrugte sal afwerp sodat die Israeliete en die Palestyne as bure vrede en sekuriteit kan geniet binne hul soewereine gebiede._ “And we hope this time that the renewed attempts by the international community to find lasting solutions for this conflict, will bear fruit in order for the Israelis and Palestinians to enjoy peace and security within their sovereign territories.”


### <a name="Neg">`Neg`</a>: negative particle

The negative particle _nie_ used in double negations.

#### Examples

* _En dus, in die woorde van voormalige President Mandela, "durf ons nie talm nie, want ons lang pad is nog nie verby <b>nie</b>"._ “And therefore, in the words of former President Mandela, "we don't dare to delay, as our long path is not over yet".”
