In Spanish, punctuation marks like question marks (¿?), exclamative marks (¡!), and quotation marks (“”) are used in pairs, with an opening mark at the beginning of the sentence or phrase and a closing mark at the end. This is different from English, where only the closing marks are used.

The two values for PunctSide in Spanish are:


### <a name="Ini">`Ini`</a>: initial

Ini is used to indicate the opening punctuation mark in a sentence or phrase.

#### Examples:

* ¿ - ¿Cómo estás?
* ¡ - ¡Hola!
* “ - “Buenos días”


### <a name="Fin">`Fin`</a>: final

Fin is used to indicate the closing punctuation mark in a sentence or phrase.

#### Examples:

* ? - ¿Cómo estás?
* ! - ¡Hola!
* ” - “Buenos días”
