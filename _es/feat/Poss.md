### <a name="Yes">`Yes`</a>: it is possessive

In Spanish, possessive pronouns and determiners indicate ownership or a relationship of belonging between the possessor and the possessed. They agree in gender and number with the noun they modify.

Spanish possessive pronouns and determiners include:
"mi" (my), "tu" (your), "su" (his, her, your formal, their), "nuestro/a" (our), "vuestro/a" (your plural)

#### Examples

* _Es mi libro._ "It's my book."
* _Es tu coche._ "It's your [singular] car."
* _Son sus zapatos._ "These are his/her/your[polite]/their shoes."
